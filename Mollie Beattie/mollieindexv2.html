Mollie Beattie

1947 - 1996

Conservationist

The Nation owes Mollie a deep debt of gratitude. In a time of unprecedented challenge to some of this Nation's most important environmental laws, Mollie stepped forward to remind us that threatened and endangered species, and the national wildlife refuges on which many of those species depend, must be protected for future generations of Americans to treasure and enjoy.

1947

Born in Glen Cove, NY.

1968

Graduated with a bachelors in Philosophy from Marymount College, Tarrytown.

1973

Traveled to Colorado, discovered her passion for the great outdoors.

1979

Graduated with a masters in forestry from the University of Vermont.

1983

Program Director for the Windham Foundation, a nonprofit organization that conducts seminars on critical issues facing Vermont.

1985

Began working as the Vermont Commissioner of Forest, Parks, and Recreation

1989

Became Deputy Secretary for Vermonts Agency of Natural Resources.

1991

Graduated with a master in public administration frmo Harvard University

1993 - 1996

Appointed first female Director of the United States Fish and Wildlife Service.

  • Defended the Endangered Species Act
  • Worked with the City of Austin to develop a habitat conservation plan to protext 30,000 acres for 8 threatened or endangered species.
  • Reintroduced the Gray Wolf, a keystone species, back into Yellowstone.

June 1996

Mollie passed away due to brain cancer.

1996

8 million acres of the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge renamed in Mollie's honor.
